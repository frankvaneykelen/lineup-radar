Le Bateau presents: Monstrous Voices @ Down The Rabbit Hole 2026

France

Bio

Using festival bio due to a lack of publicly available data

The French artist duo Le Bateau designed the floating sculpture 'Monstrous Voices' on the lake specifically for Down The Rabbit Hole and the water art festival Bosch Parade. The elegant wooden ship is inspired by the mythological sea monster Scylla and consists of countless individual parts, which were cut out by Laëtitia Delauney, Aurélia Noudelmann, and a crew of predominantly female and queer volunteers. Spectacular in itself, but as an additional eye-catcher, the seaworthy boat also features four graceful oars, which will soon be set in motion in the Groene Heuvels in a très clever way by singing voices. Interested in helping build it? Until the end of May, you’re more than welcome to contribute to the project at Buurtwerkplaats Noorderhof in Amsterdam. By doing so, you’ll have a chance to win two festival tickets for DTRH26! More information about the project and how to sign up can be found on this page.

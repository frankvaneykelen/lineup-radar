Bio

Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark (OMD) are a pioneering synth-pop band from the UK, formed in 1978 by Andy McCluskey and Paul Humphreys. Known for their innovative use of synthesizers, they achieved critical and commercial success with hits like 'Enola Gay,' 'If You Leave,' and 'Electricity,' becoming one of the most influential bands in the electronic music genre.

Festival Bio (English)

OMD, the influential synthpop duo from Wirral, England, combined bold experimentation with massive hit-making ability on albums like ‘Organisation, Architecture and Morality’ and ‘Dazzle Ships.’ With classics such as ‘Enola Gay,’ ‘Maid of Orleans,’ and ‘Souvenir,’ they defined the sound of the 1980s. After celebrating their 40th anniversary, they returned with ‘Bauhaus Staircase’ (2023), their most politically charged and artistically daring album in years, which also brought the duo to perform at AFAS Live in Amsterdam. OMD’s songs are expansive, electronic sonic masterpieces with lyrics that grab you by the throat.

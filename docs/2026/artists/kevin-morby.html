About (from Festival)

A song by Kevin Morby is the musical equivalent of flipping through a photo album filled with old Polaroids. The singer-songwriter, who grew up in Kansas and moved to New York, always has one foot in the past. This makes him one of the few contemporary musicians who truly deserves comparisons to Bob Dylan and Leonard Cohen. However, it’s not pure nostalgia: in his simmering songs, this troubadour reflects on our tumultuous times. They are passionate odes to the desolate places and lonely people of the modern world. Een nummer van Kevin Morby is het muzikale equivalent van door een fotoalbum met oude polaroids bladeren. Pure nostalgie is het overigens niet: in zijn broeierige songs reflecteert deze troubadour op onze tumultueuze tijden.

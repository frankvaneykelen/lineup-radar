Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about LineupRadar

General Questions

What is LineupRadar?

LineupRadar is a festival lineup discovery tool that helps you explore complete artist lineups with detailed information including ratings, genres, bios, and metadata. It's designed to help you discover hidden gems and plan your perfect festival experience.

Which festivals are currently tracked?

We currently track three major European festivals:

  • Down The Rabbit Hole (Beuningen, Netherlands) - 39 artists for 2026
  • Pinkpop (Landgraaf, Netherlands) - 32 artists for 2026
  • Rock Werchter (Werchter, Belgium) - 29 artists for 2026

Features & Functionality

How do I filter artists?

Each festival lineup page includes multiple filter options:

  • Genre Filter: Select specific music genres
  • Country Filter: Filter by artist country of origin
  • Rating Filter: View only highly-rated artists (9+, 8+, 7+, 6+)
  • Gender Filter: Filter by gender of front person (Male, Female, Mixed, Non-binary, Unknown)
  • Person of Color Filter: Filter by whether the front person is a person of color
  • Search Box: Search across all fields including artist names, genres, and bios

Can I sort the artist table?

Yes! Click on any column header with an arrow icon to sort by that column. Click again to reverse the sort order.

What information is available for each artist?

Each artist page includes:

  • Bio: Background information about the artist
  • Genre: Musical style and genre tags
  • Country: Artist's country of origin
  • Rating: Critic-based rating (1-10 scale)
  • Group Size: Number of people in the act
  • Demographics: Gender and diversity information
  • Images: Artist photos from festival websites
  • Social Links: Links to Spotify, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube
  • My Take: Critical assessment and live performance notes

Does LineupRadar have a dark mode?

Yes! Click the moon/sun icon in the footer to toggle between light and dark modes. Your preference will be saved automatically.

Data & Accuracy

Where does the data come from?

Data comes from multiple sources:

  • Festival Websites: Official lineups, bios, and images are scraped from festival websites
  • AI Enhancement: Azure OpenAI (GPT-4o-mini) enriches data with genres, ratings, and critical assessments
  • Spotify: Fallback for artist images when festival images aren't available

How accurate is the information?

While we strive for accuracy, please note that some content is AI-generated and may contain inaccuracies. Always verify critical details on official festival websites or artist pages. The disclaimer at the bottom of each page provides more information.

How are ratings determined?

Ratings (1-10 scale) are based on critical consensus, including album reviews, live performance assessments, and industry recognition. They're intended to help you identify highly-regarded artists you might not know yet.

How often is the data updated?

Data is updated when new artists are announced by festivals. The lineups reflect the most recent announcements at the time of generation. Current update status:

  • Down The Rabbit Hole 2026: Last updated December 03, 2025 20:31 UTC
  • Pinkpop 2026: Last updated December 03, 2025 21:58 UTC
  • Rock Werchter 2026: Last updated December 03, 2025 20:30 UTC

Each lineup page also displays its last update timestamp in the header.

Technical Questions

Is LineupRadar mobile-friendly?

Yes! The site is fully responsive and works on mobile devices, tablets, and desktops. On mobile portrait mode, you'll see a message suggesting landscape orientation for the best table viewing experience.

Can I link directly to a specific artist?

Yes! Each artist has their own dedicated page with a permanent URL that you can share or bookmark.

Is the source code available?

Yes! LineupRadar is open source. You can view the code and contribute on GitHub.

Contact & Feedback

I found an error. How do I report it?

Please report issues on our GitHub Issues page. We appreciate your help in improving the accuracy of the data!

Can you add my favorite festival?

Festival requests can be made via GitHub Issues. We prioritize indie and boutique festivals with publicly available lineup data.

How can I support this project?

The best way to support LineupRadar is to:

  • Star the project on GitHub
